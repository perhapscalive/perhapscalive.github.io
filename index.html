About Me

I hold a Master's degree in Hydrology, specializing in the development and application of advanced numerical models. My work bridges fundamental hydrological processes with applied environmental engineering, focusing on hydrodynamic modeling, catchment-scale water balances, and the effects of large-scale climate change on aquatic systems.

Currently, I am deeply interested in leveraging both physics-based models and data-driven approaches to simulate complex environmental flows and extreme hydrological events, with the aim of providing rigorous, rigorous insights into how our water resources respond to a changing environment.

Hydrodynamics

My hydrodynamics research involves high-fidelity 3D modeling of open channel flows, coastal dynamics, and localized turbulent structures.

Interactive 3D Hydrodynamics Visualizer

We developed a web-based, real-time 3D visualization tool for massive unstructured mesh hydrodynamic simulations. It renders dynamic water surface elevations and flow fields directly in the browser.

The viewer features academic-grade colormaps for depth variation and interactive camera controls, built on top of WebGL and Three.js technologies. This aids in rapid data interpretation for environmental engineering projects.

High-Fidelity Coastal Modeling

Investigating long-term morphological changes and wave-current interactions in complex coastal regions using coupled 3D numerical models.

This project emphasizes the role of localized turbulence and bed shear stress in sediment transport, validating numerical predictions against large-scale laboratory flume experiments.

Turbulent Flow Structures in Open Channels

Large Eddy Simulation (LES) of turbulent structures and secondary flows in rough-bed open channels. Our modeling captures the intricate flow field variations over complex riverbeds.

Hydrology

In the field of hydrology, my focus lies in understanding catchment-scale water balances, extreme event forecasting (floods and droughts), and the impact of anthropogenic activities on regional water resources.

Catchment Modeling and Runoff Predictions

Developing integrated physics-based models combined with statistical tools to forecast streamflow and assess water availability in ungauged basins.

Climate Change Impacts on Water Cycles

Evaluating the long-term impacts of climate shifts on extreme hydrological events using statistical downscaling of Global Climate Models (GCMs) and regional hydrological routing.

Tools & Datasets

Committed to open science, I actively develop and maintain several open-source tools for the environmental engineering community.

HydroVis-WebGL Toolkit

A lightweight, browser-based visualizer for massive unstructured mesh hydrodynamic outputs. Supports dynamic rendering, interactive temporal playback, and custom academic colormaps without requiring standalone software like Paraview or Tecplot.

Catchment-ML Toolkit

A Python-based framework tailored for applying various machine learning models (e.g., LSTM, Random Forest) to ungauged basin runoff predictions and streamflow reconstruction across global catchments.
